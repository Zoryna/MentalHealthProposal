What Can You Do?

  • Apply to Mentors for Mental Health
  • Start a mental health awareness club or event. This will educate students about the importance of mental health, and it can give students a place they feel comfortable to discuss mental health issues.

Tips on how to start a discussion about mental health

  • Ask leading questions by asking them how they feel
  • Express that you are there to listen and that you want them to be comfortable
  • Offer that they are allowed to discuss this with someone else
  • Communicate that you are concerned about them

Tips on how to discuss mental health with your child

  • Have the discussion when your child is comfortable
  • Be aware of their reactions to see if they are uncomfortable or upset
  • Communicate in a straightforward way
  • Make sure you listen

    How to notice if a friend is not acting like theirself

  • Notice if their behavior differs from their normal behavior
  • Pay attenion if they are more withdrawn from other people
  • Be wary of how they speak and what they talk about, if it is mostly negative or positive topics

    • How to talk to your friends about mental illness

  • Regularly ask them how they are doing
  • Mention that you notice something different about them
  • Offer to listen if it seems like something is bothering them and make sure to be understanding and non-judgemental

    • Know when to ask for help from a professional

  • If you are experiencing something that affects your ability to function, seek a professional
  • If you want to improve yourself
  • If you simply want to see a professional for concern over your health, you are allowed to do so
