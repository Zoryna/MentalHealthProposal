Mentors for Mental Health

Mentors for Mental Health is a one-week mental health training program. It trains high school staff and high school upperclassmen of participating schools to detect mental illnesses in students and possibly help them find some professional help. Our program will educate all participants, but it will focus on teachers, student peers, and security guards. It will offer informational courses on abnormal psychology, social psychology, and the specific challenges that students with low socioeconomic status face. It will also train them on how to approach students and offer support if they seem to be showing some symptoms of a mental illness.
