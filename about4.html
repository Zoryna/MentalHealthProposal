Frequently Asked Questions

Only 20 low-income schools in your city or town are eligible. Our program is offered to low-income schools since they do not recieve enough funding to have many mental health resources for their students. Schools must apply and be approved before 50 or fewer school staff are able to receive our training.

We train teachers because they are staff that interact with students the most. However, we train security because there are many cases where security guards mishandle or abuse students with mental illnesses. Security can avoid mishandling students if they are aware that they may have mental health issues. Upperclassmen, 10 juniors and 10 seniors, are allowed to receive training if they and their parents consent them to do so. It is important for students to have peers who understand mental illnesses.

Training will take place over the summer during July. We chose the training to be set in July because it is near the time when some schools begin their school year. By July, some schools and teachers will have their curriculum prepared, so our training will not interfere with their planning. Since our training is closer to when a school year starts, staff and volunteering upperclassmen will be prepared to apply what they have learned.

The lectures will be conducted by psychology professionals, which may be psychologists, psychology professors, or psychiatrists. They will choose the material and organize the lectures.

Yes! We are a free program because we are aware that low-income schools do not receive enough funding to support their students.
